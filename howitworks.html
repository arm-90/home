How it works
1 /
Fixate’s AI solution can analyse any visual content, helping designers, brands and marketers to measure the quality and effectiveness of their work objectively and with confidence - with just a bit of help from science. Fixate starts with you simply uploading images or videos. We then use our suite of algorithms and machine learning to do three things:
1

Forecast user attention patterns.

2

Predict which elements of your creative will gain the most attention.

3

Show you where you might consider improvements based on data-driven results.

With this tool designers can quickly and accurately analyse any image or video; so now it’s easier than ever to know what should capture the audience’s attention!
1

We handpicked millions of images and videos. They cover - design, advertising, social media, packaging and merchandising etc. They represent an extremely wide range of scenarios.

2

We used more than one million images to learn what catches people's attention. To do this we used eye-tracking technology.

3

We used that eye-tracking data and deep learning algorithms to train AI models.

4

5

Our AI solution powers an application that you can use on your computer, tablet, or smartphone.

6

We offer a subscription service where people can use the app themselves. We also have plugins for Sketch and Chrome that designers can use, and we have an API for when businesses want to build something or connect with their own apps.

We used this large dataset of videos and images to learn what catches people's attention. To do this we used eye-tracking technology.
We used that eye-tracking data and deep learning algorithms to train AI models.
Our model not only learns the location of static object fixations for each frame of the video but also assigns attention priority to moving objects. For non-static scenes, our method closely mimics how humans view them. Even to the point where we continue staring at a point in the frame when there is no longer motion. Our model is highly correlated with real eye tracking studies and takes minutes instead of weeks to produce results that are just as accurate!

Original Image
Original image or video uploaded for analysis by user
Heat Map
Heat maps reveal a user's level of attention to specific areas of an image while they are viewing it.
Clarity Map
A clarity map is how much people see of an image. When there are many items that draw attention, the map is not clear.
Most Salient Objects
Visual saliency is a way to describe the most “eye-catching” parts of a scene or image.
Areas of Interest
Areas of interest show how much attention different defined areas in an image have.
Clarity Areas of Interest
Clarity Areas of interest show clarity levels for different defined areas in an image have.
Text Score
Text Scores show how much attention any text receives in the image.
Face Score
Face Scores show how much attention any faces detected in an image recieves.
Text Clarity
Text clarity shows the clarity share of any text detected in the image.
Face Clarity
Face clarity shows the clarity share of any face detected in the image.
Clarity and Complexity Score
Higher clarity scores are achieved when a single or very few narrow areas draw attention. When images are more complex, they lead to more perceptual load and thereby more cognitive demand making it harder to understand what is being viewed.
